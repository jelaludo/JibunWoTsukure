# Animation Storyboard 01 — Kuzushi Is Not Only Off-Balancing

## Goal

Explain that **Kuzushi is broader than off-balancing** and introduce three forms of disruption:

- **Balance**
- **Structure**
- **Alignment**

Introduce the deeper principle:

**自分を作れ — Build Yourself**

Target length: **2–3 minutes**

Style: **conceptual animation**, inspired by 3Blue1Brown. Minimal geometry, clear causal motion, one idea per motion.

---

## Visual Grammar

### Minimal mannequin
- **Head**: circle
- **Spine**: vertical line
- **Shoulders**: horizontal bar
- **Hips**: horizontal bar
- **Feet**: two points
- **Base of support**: polygon on the ground
- **Center of gravity**: glowing dot

### Additional elements
- **Frame limbs**: rigid bars
- **Collapsed frames**: bent lines
- **Alignment**: shoulder axis vs hip axis
- **Pressure**: arrows

### Color conventions
- **You**: cool blue
- **Opponent**: warm orange
- **Stable**: solid bright lines
- **Unstable**: dim or wobbling lines

---

## Scene 1 — The Traditional View

### Purpose
Present the common definition: Kuzushi = off-balancing.

### Visual
A mannequin stands upright with:
- visible base polygon
- visible center-of-gravity dot

On-screen text:

> Kuzushi = Off-balancing

### Motion
- Opponent pushes
- Center-of-gravity dot slides outside the base polygon
- Base flashes red
- Figure tilts and falls

### Teaching point
This is the **traditional interpretation** of Kuzushi.

### Beat
Pause after the fall.

---

## Scene 2 — Questioning the Definition

### Purpose
Challenge the idea that off-balancing is the whole story.

### Visual
Reset. Both grapplers are now on the ground.

On-screen text:

> But is that the whole story?

### Motion
- Top player rotates opponent’s head sideways
- Shoulder line rotates
- Hip line remains fixed
- An angle indicator appears between shoulders and hips
- Opponent’s torso twists

Highlight:
- alignment axis glows red

On-screen text:

> Alignment disrupted

### Teaching point
The opponent is compromised **without losing balance** in the classic standing sense.

---

## Scene 3 — Structural Collapse

### Purpose
Show a second non-balance form of Kuzushi.

### Visual
Reset. Bottom player posts with two arm frames.
Arms are shown as rigid straight bars.

### Motion
- Pressure arrow appears
- Pressure increases
- Frame bars bend inward
- Torso compresses

On-screen text:

> Structure collapsed

### Teaching point
The opponent loses structural integrity **without traditional off-balancing**.

---

## Scene 4 — Expanding Kuzushi

### Purpose
Introduce the full taxonomy.

### Visual
Screen clears. Triangle diagram appears:

```text
             Alignment
               ▲
               │
               │
Structure ◄────┼────► Balance
```

Center label:

> Kuzushi

### Motion
Each vertex lights up as a short replay clip appears.

#### Balance
Replay the standing off-balance moment.

#### Structure
Replay the frame-collapse moment.

#### Alignment
Replay the head-twist moment.

On-screen text:

> Kuzushi = Disruption

### Teaching point
Kuzushi should be understood more broadly as **disruption**, not only off-balancing.

---

## Scene 5 — Two Body States

### Purpose
Shift from isolated examples to a relational model.

### Visual
Split screen.

Left label:
> You

Right label:
> Opponent

Under each figure: three bars
- Balance
- Structure
- Alignment

Bars initially start full.

### Motion
- You establish head position
- Opponent alignment bar drops
- Pressure is applied
- Opponent structure bar drops
- Angle is created
- Opponent balance bar collapses

Technique label appears at the end:

> Back Take

### Teaching point
Techniques succeed when **structural asymmetry** appears.

---

## Scene 6 — Build Yourself

### Purpose
Introduce self-structure as the primary variable.

### Visual
All elements fade except one mannequin.
The body initially appears slightly misaligned:
- shoulders uneven
- base narrow
- center of gravity unstable

### Motion
The body gradually organizes:
- spine stacks
- shoulders align
- base widens
- center of gravity settles
- frames become straight and bright

On-screen text:

**自分を作れ**  
**Build Yourself**

### Teaching point
Before breaking the opponent, one must build one’s own usable structure.

---

## Scene 7 — Judo Doctrine Reframed

### Purpose
Reframe the familiar sequence.

### Visual
Three words appear:

- Break
- Set
- Execute

Then they rearrange into:

- Set
- Break
- Execute

### Motion
- Self structure stabilizes
- Opponent triangle begins collapsing
- Throw arc appears
- Opponent falls

### Teaching point
The deeper logic is:

> Build yourself → Break them → Execute

---

## Scene 8 — Final Concept

### Purpose
End with the full model.

### Visual
Two triangles appear.

Left:
> Your Structure

Right:
> Opponent Structure

Opponent triangle collapses.

Final on-screen text:

> Grappling is the management of two structures.

Final line:

> Build yourself.  
> Break them.  
> Execute.

---

## Animation Rhythm

Recommended pacing pattern:

1. show  
2. pause  
3. question  
4. show contradiction  
5. expand concept  
6. resolve

---

## Core Message

Kuzushi is not only off-balancing.

Kuzushi is **disruption of the opponent’s structure**, which can occur through:

- Balance
- Structure
- Alignment

The deeper principle is:

**Build Yourself first.**
